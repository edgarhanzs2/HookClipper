1. Introduction

Welcome to AI Hook Clipper! This application uses cutting-edge artificial intelligence to analyze your videos and automatically find the most engaging, viral-worthy moments — called "hooks." It then generates short clips optimized for social media platforms like TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

With AI Hook Clipper, you can transform a long video into multiple shareable clips in just minutes, complete with professional subtitles and vertical formatting.

3. Getting Started

1

Open your browser and navigate to the application URL (e.g., http://localhost:3000 for local development).

2

Choose Your Input Method

On the home page, choose between "Upload a Video" or "Paste YouTube URL."

3

Select an AI Provider

Choose your preferred AI provider from the dropdown: OpenAI (cloud), Gemini (cloud), or Ollama (local/offline).

Wait for Processing

The AI will analyze your video through a multi-step pipeline. Progress is shown in real-time with animations and fun facts.

4. Uploading a Video

Using Drag and Drop

  1. Open the application in your browser.
  2. Drag your video file from your file manager onto the upload zone.
  3. The upload zone will highlight in purple with a "Drop to upload" message.
  4. Release the file to start the upload.

Using the File Browser

  1. Click on the upload zone area.
  2. A file browser dialog will appear.
  3. Navigate to and select your video file.
  4. Click "Open" to start the upload.

Supported Formats

  • MP4 (recommended)
  • MOV
  • AVI
  • MKV
  • WebM
💡 Tip

For best results, use MP4 files with H.264 encoding. Maximum recommended file size is 500 MB.

5. Using YouTube URLs

Instead of uploading a file, you can process any public YouTube video by pasting its URL.

How to Use

  1. Copy the URL of a YouTube video from your browser.
  2. Switch to the "YouTube URL" tab on the home page.
  3. Paste the URL into the input field.
  4. Click "Process" to start downloading and analyzing.

Supported URL Formats

  • Standard: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VIDEO_ID
  • Short: https://youtu.be/VIDEO_ID
  • Shorts: https://www.youtube.com/shorts/VIDEO_ID
⚠️ Note

Videos are downloaded at 720p quality. Private or age-restricted videos cannot be downloaded.

8. Viewing Results

Once processing is complete, the results page displays all detected hook clips as cards.

Clip Card Information

Each clip card shows:

  • Title — AI-generated descriptive name for the hook.
  • Score — Virality score from 0-100 (higher = more likely to go viral).
  • Duration — Length of the clip in seconds.
  • Timestamp — Start and end times in the original video.
  • Reason — AI explanation of why this moment was selected.
  • Video Preview — Inline video player to preview the clip.
💡 Tip

Clips are sorted by virality score (highest first). The best hooks appear at the top.

9. Customizing Clips

Re-Trimming Clips

If the AI's timestamp selection isn't perfect, you can manually adjust the clip boundaries:

  1. Click the timeline editor icon on a clip card.
  2. Drag the start and end handles to adjust boundaries.
  3. Preview the adjustment in real-time.
  4. Click "Apply" to re-trim with your custom timestamps.

Timeline Editor

The timeline editor provides an intuitive visual interface for fine-tuning clip boundaries. Drag handles on either end of the selection region to adjust start and end points. The current selection is highlighted in purple, with timestamps displayed above.

10. Rendering Vertical Videos

Convert any clip to 9:16 vertical format optimized for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

How to Render

  1. Click the "Render Vertical" button on any clip card.
  2. A style picker modal will appear with three subtitle options.
  3. Select your preferred subtitle style.
  4. Click "Render" to start processing.
  5. The vertical version generates with face-tracking crop and subtitles.
  6. Once complete, a download button appears for the vertical clip.

Face Tracking

When rendering vertical videos, MediaPipe face detection locates the speaker's face and centers the 9:16 crop window on it, ensuring the speaker is always visible. If no face is detected, the crop defaults to center position.

11. Downloading Clips

Download Original Clip

Click the "Download" button on any clip card to download the clip in its original landscape format as an MP4 file.

Download Vertical Clip

After rendering a vertical version, click the "Download Vertical" button to download the 9:16 vertical clip with face-tracked cropping and burned-in subtitles.

File Naming

Clips are automatically named with the job ID and clip number (e.g., abc123_clip_1.mp4). Vertical clips include a _vertical suffix.

12. Troubleshooting

❌ Video upload fails
Ensure the file is a supported format (MP4, MOV, AVI, MKV, WebM) and under 500 MB. Check that the backend server is running.
❌ YouTube URL is rejected
Verify the URL is a valid YouTube link. Ensure the video is public and not age-restricted.
❌ Processing stalls or hangs
Check that the Celery worker and Redis server are running. Look at the Celery worker terminal for error messages.
❌ No hooks detected
Try a different AI provider. Some videos with minimal speech may not produce hooks. Ensure the video has clear audio.
❌ Vertical render fails
Ensure FFmpeg is installed and up to date. Check that the clip exists and hasn't been deleted.
❌ Worker crashes on macOS (SIGABRT)
Use --pool=solo when starting the Celery worker: celery -A celery_app.celery worker --loglevel=info --pool=solo
❌ ModuleNotFoundError in Celery
Start the Celery worker from the backend/ directory. Clear __pycache__ and restart the worker.
❌ Ollama returns empty response
The transcript may exceed the model's context limit. The system auto-truncates, but very long videos may lose some content. Try a shorter video.

13. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does processing take? ▼

Typically 1-5 minutes depending on video length and your AI provider. Shorter videos (under 10 minutes) process fastest.

Is my video stored permanently? ▼

Videos and clips are stored on the server temporarily. They are not automatically deleted but are meant for session use. For production, configure cloud storage with retention policies.

Can I process multiple videos at once? ▼

Yes! Each video gets its own job ID. You can submit multiple videos and track each one independently.

Which AI provider should I use? ▼

OpenAI offers the best quality. Gemini has a generous free tier. Ollama is best for privacy and offline use.

What is Mock AI mode? ▼

Mock AI mode skips actual AI processing and returns dummy data. Useful for testing the application without consuming API credits.

Can I use this commercially? ▼

The application is open-source. Ensure you comply with AI provider terms of service and respect copyright when processing third-party videos.

Do subtitles support multiple languages? ▼

Transcription supports the languages of the selected AI provider. OpenAI Whisper supports 90+ languages.

What video quality is output? ▼

Clips are encoded with H.264 video and AAC audio at CRF 23 (visually lossless). Vertical clips are output at 1080×1920 resolution.
